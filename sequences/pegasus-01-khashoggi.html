Episode II — Pegasus, Case I

A journalist walked into a building. His phone was already compromised.

What is Pegasus?

Pegasus is military-grade spyware developed by NSO Group, an Israeli cyber-intelligence firm founded in 2010 by veterans of Unit 8200 — Israel's equivalent of the NSA. Once installed on a phone, Pegasus grants the operator complete access: messages (including WhatsApp, Signal, and Telegram), photos, passwords, GPS location, the microphone, the camera, and every keystroke. All without the user knowing.

It is deployed via zero-click exploits — no link to tap, no attachment to open. A missed iMessage or WhatsApp call is enough to compromise the device. The user takes no action. The infection is invisible.

NSO Group sells Pegasus exclusively to government agencies. A single license costs millions. But the company has repeatedly sold to regimes with documented records of targeting journalists, activists, and political opponents — Saudi Arabia, UAE, India, Hungary, Morocco, Mexico, Azerbaijan, and others.FSCLPZ

The Khashoggi case

Jamal Khashoggi was a Saudi journalist, a columnist for The Washington Post, and a US permanent resident. He was one of the most prominent critics of Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman. On October 2, 2018, at 13:14 local time, he entered the Saudi consulate in Istanbul to obtain divorce paperwork. His fiancée Hatice Cengiz waited outside. He never emerged.

But Khashoggi had been under surveillance long before he walked through that door. Pegasus had already been deployed against his inner circle months earlier.

His wife Hanan Elatr's phone was targeted in the months leading up to the killing. After October 2, the targeting expanded: four days after the murder, Cengiz's phone was infected with Pegasus. Weeks later, Khashoggi's son Abdullah was added to the target list.

The operator deployed the exploit by impersonating The Washington Post — using fake Washington Post domains and links to trick targets into opening the infection vector. A press outlet was weaponized against the family of one of its own journalists.

"We assess that Saudi Arabia's Crown Prince Muhammad bin Salman approved an operation in Istanbul, Turkey to capture or kill Saudi journalist Jamal Khashoggi." — US Office of the Director of National Intelligence, February 26, 2021ODNI

The global scale

Khashoggi was not an isolated target. In July 2021, The Pegasus Project — an investigation coordinated by Forbidden Stories and Amnesty International involving 80+ journalists from 17 media organizations — obtained a leaked database of 50,000 phone numbers selected for Pegasus surveillance across more than 50 countries.

At least 180 journalists across 20 countries appeared on the list. Morocco alone had 10,000+ numbers selected. Mexico: 15,000. Heads of state, including Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez and Defence Minister Margarita Robles, were infected.

Amnesty International's Security Lab examined 67 phones and confirmed 23 successful Pegasus infections with 14 additional signs of attempted penetration — an 85% confirmation rate on devices where forensic analysis was possible.AICL

The arms race

Apple and NSO Group have been in a continuous arms race since at least 2018. Each time Apple patches a vulnerability, NSO finds a new one:

2019: WhatsApp calling exploit (CVE-2019-3568) — 1,400 phones targeted in two weeks.

2020: KISMET exploit against iOS 13.5.1 via iMessage.

2021: FORCEDENTRY — the most sophisticated. Sent via a malicious .gif that wasn't actually a .gif. It exploited the JBIG2 image codec to bypass Apple's Pointer Authentication Codes and disable ASLR. Apple responded with BlastDoor — a sandbox for iMessage data.

2023: BLASTPASS/BLASTPAST — NSO bypassed BlastDoor using PassKit attachments with embedded WebP images. The exploit chained through libwebp (CVE-2023-4863).

Eight documented iOS exploit chains in four years. The phone in your pocket is a battlefield.CLCL22PZ25ASL

The company that kept selling

In November 2021, the US Commerce Department placed NSO Group on the Entity List — banning American companies from selling technology to the firm. The stated reason: "Developed and supplied spyware to foreign governments that used these tools to maliciously target government officials, journalists, businesspeople, activists, academics, and embassy workers."BIS

Yet NSO continued to operate. In court filings, the company admitted that Pegasus represented "100 percent of NSO's sales in 2025." One product. One purpose.

In October 2025, NSO Group was acquired by US investors led by Hollywood producer Robert Simonds. The restructuring shed approximately $600 million in debt. All three co-founders — Niv Karmi, Shalev Hulio, and Omri Lavie — lost their shares.TC

In November 2025, David Friedman was appointed Chairman. Friedman is the former US Ambassador to Israel under the Trump administration. NSO court filings now state that "it is reasonably foreseeable that a law enforcement or intelligence agency of the United States will use Pegasus."TOI

In June 2026 — just weeks ago — Meta filed a contempt motion, accusing NSO of violating a permanent injunction by launching new spear-phishing attempts and test accounts on WhatsApp. The spyware kept flying.ArsMeta
